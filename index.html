Muslims worldwide unite in a festive celebration of Eid al-Fitr as the crescent moon illuminates the night sky, signaling the end of Ramadan. Known as the "Festival of Breaking the Fast," this essential Islamic holiday is a time for solidarity, introspection, and thankfulness. In the Philippines, where an array of cultures lives side by side, Eid al-Fitr is a national festival with special significance for the Muslim community. It highlights the nation's dedication to religious and cultural peace. Eid al-Fitr is celebrated after a month of prayer, fasting, and spiritual discipline known as Ramadan. Muslims use this time to firmly establish their religion, practice self-control, and purify themselves. As the holy month draws to a close, Eid is a time to rejoice and thank Allah for the strength and determination displayed during the fast.
